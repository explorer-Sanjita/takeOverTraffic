We’re Changing the Way the INDIA Thinks About TRAFFIC & ROAD SAFETY

Road safety remains a major development issue, a public health concern and a leading cause of death and injury worldwide. The costs of road accidents are borne not only by victims and their families, but also by the entire economy in the form of premature death, injury, disability and potential loss of income.

Why Traffic Management?

According to the World Health Organization, at least one in 10 road deaths worldwide is from India. 

About 15,000 people die on Indian roads each year, averaging 1130 accidents and 422 deaths per day, or 47 accidents and 18 deaths per hour. increase. In 2021, a total of 4,12,432 road accidents were reported across the country, killing 1,53,972 people and injuring 3,84,448 people. Unfortunately, the age group most affected in road crashes is the 18-45 year old, who accounts for about 67% of all crash fatalities.
